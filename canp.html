Dr. Charles Allen Nature Preserve

Background

The Charles Allen Nature Preserve is an 80-acre outdoor classroom located in the rolling Ouachita Hills Region near Columbia, Louisiana, about 30 miles from ULM.

The property was donated to ULM in 2000 by Dr. Harry Winters, in honor of Dr. Charles Allen, a former ULM professor and renowned botanist in Louisiana. Dr. Winters and Dr. Allen have co-authored several books on Louisiana and Gulf South plant species, and the Charles Allen Nature Preserve serves as one of their greatest contributions to conservation.

Efforts are currently underway to improve accessibility for students and researchers. The goal is to transform this biological station into a premier research site for scientists across Louisiana and beyond.
