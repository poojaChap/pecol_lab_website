Undergraduate Students


Subash Sapkota


A senior undergraduate student from Nepal pursuing a dual major in Biology and Mathematics, Subash is passionate about combating climate change through innovative ecosystem monitoring. He specializes in integrating modern technologies like remote sensing and machine learning to enhance environmental protection efforts.

Research Focus:


  • - Multispectral/hyperspectral remote sensing for ecosystem assessment
  • - Machine learning applications in environmental monitoring
  • - Biodiversity analysis using spectral data
  • - Forest dynamics and regeneration patterns
  • - Climate change impact mitigation strategies


Key Projects:


Aquatic Plant Phenology Tracking (ULM Plant Ecology Lab)

Developed UAV-based machine learning models to identify aquatic species and monitor phenological spread patterns

Northwoods Seedling Distribution Analysis (UNDERC 2023)

Investigated ecological and topographical drivers of seedling patterns in boreal forests

Hyperspectral Biodiversity Assessment (UNDERC 2024)

Analyzing NEON data to evaluate taxonomic, phylogenetic, and functional diversity using spectral signatures

Current Initiative:


Leading LiDAR integration research analyzing vertical forest stratification and regeneration potential in mixed hardwood stands

Beyond the Lab:


Enjoys exploring cultural music from Nepal to Nashville, strategic chess matches, wilderness hiking, and analyzing sports/film narratives
